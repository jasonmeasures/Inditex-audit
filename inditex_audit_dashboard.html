EU Cap Logic — Why ES/PT Items Resolve to Either 9903.02.19 or 9903.02.20

AuthorityEO 14326 § 2(b) (Aug 5, 2025) MechanismFor an EU good, if the Column 1 (MFN) rate ≥ 15%, the additional ad valorem duty is 0% (code 9903.02.19). If MFN < 15%, the additional duty is set so the total reaches 15% — applied as an additive 15% via code 9903.02.20. In this entryAll Spain/Portugal goods are apparel HTS 6203/6204/etc. with MFN rates typically <15%, so 9903.02.20 (+15%) applies. The engine selects per-line based on the filed MFN rate. EffectEU shipments pay a flat 15% landed reciprocal rate, never more.

China Stack — Why CN Has Three Layers

Layer 1 (Section 301)One of four mutually exclusive codes — each HTS is on exactly one list. Not affected by SCOTUS ruling — Section 301 is independently authorized under Trade Act of 1974 § 301.
 • 9903.88.01 List 1 +25% (machinery, electronics) · eff. Jul 6, 2018
 • 9903.88.02 List 2 +25% (semiconductors, chemicals) · eff. Aug 23, 2018
 • 9903.88.03 List 3 +25% (furniture, auto parts, many Ch 50–65 textile codes) · eff. Sep 24, 2018
 • 9903.88.15 List 4A +7.5% (consumer goods, most apparel/footwear) · eff. Sep 1, 2019
Engine displays 9903.88.15 as the default; accepts any 9903.88.* as satisfying coverage. Layer 29903.01.24 — IEEPA China/HK fentanyl (+10%). EO 14195 as amended Nov 10, 2025 (reduced from 20% to 10%). Layer 39903.01.25 — IEEPA Universal baseline (+10%). EO 14257. Stack ruleAll three layers apply additively to the same entered value base — no compounding. Effective China rate (List 4A example) = MFN + 7.5% + 10% + 10% = MFN + 27.5%. For List 3 HTS codes the Section 301 component is +25% instead of +7.5%.
